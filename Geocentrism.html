What is Geocentrism?

Geocentrism is an outdated cosmological model where the Earth is considered the center of the universe,
and the Sun, Moon, planets, and stars revolve around it

Demo created by Malin Christersson

Background

  • Ancient Greek origins: Early Greek thinkers like Plato and Aristotle established
    geocentric models, which were based on the obvious observation that the Sun, Moon, and stars
    appeared to move across the sky,orbiting a stationary Earth. They also believed that
    celestial objects had to move in perfect, uniform circular motions.

Ptolemaic system

  • The geocentric model was most thoroughly developed by Claudius Ptolemaeus in the 2nd century CE,
    in his work known as the Almagest. To account for the complex, non-uniform motions of the planets
    (like their retrograde motion), Ptolemy used a complex system of epicycles and deferents,
    which are circles upon circles,to accurately predict the positions of celestial bodies.
